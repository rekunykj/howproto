Holy Paladin Gear

As with most MMO-RPG's World of Warcraft is not unique in that the primary source of progressing your character comes from getting new gear. As a Holy Paladin you will be targeting primarily plate pieces of gear due to the bonus you gain from wearing a full set of plate gear. Below we will go over some recommendations for gear pieces to look out for as well outline an overall strategy for what pieces of gear to equip and why.

Stats

Gear in World of Warcraft is primarily broken down into two categories. Primary stats and secondary stats.

Primary Stats

There are three primary stats that are relevant to you as a Holy Paladin. Armor, Stamina and Intellect. Intellect is automatically converted between Strength and Intellect depending on if you are in Holy or Retribution/Protection Specialization, so we will only cover Intellect in this guide.

  • Armor - This stat reduces the physical damage you take, all gear has armor on it, and as a Plate user you get more armor on your gear than other classes.
  • Stamina - Stamina will increase your maximum health, and thus the amount of damage you can take before you die. All gear has stamina.
  • Intellect - This is the primary stat that scales your power, all gear you equip will have intellect on it and the more you have the harder your spells will hit/heal for.

Secondary Stats

There are four primary secondary stats that each enhance your character in different ways. As your gear and item level increase you will gain more and more secondary stats. Unlike primary stats these will impact how you play the game in a more meaningful way than just increasing the numbers of your abilities.

  • Critical Strike - Critical Strike chance gives you a flat % chance to deal double damage with an ability. If you have 5% Critical Strike chance, and heal for 100. 5% of the time your heals will heal for 200 instead.
  • Haste - Haste gives you a flat % increase to the cast time of your spells. It also reduces the GCD (Global Cooldown) by the same percentage. This means that if you have 10% haste all your core spells will be 10% faster, meaning you get 10% more uses out of them in the same amount of time. This does not affect all spells, cooldowns generally are not affected by Haste.
  • Mastery - The paladin mastery is called Lightbringer. It increases the healing you do to a target by a flat % based on how close your character is in relation to them. If you are right ontop of each other you get 100% benefit. If you are 20yd's away you get 50% and 0% at 40yds away.
  • Versatility - Versatility is the simplest of secondary stats. It increases all healing/damage you do by a flat % and reduces the damage you take by half of that. If you have 5% versatility all of your spells will heal for 5% more, and you will take 2.5% less damage.

Stat Weights

It is not uncommon to see the term stat weight's mentioned. Stat weight's are a way of determining a value of a piece of gear by associating a numerical value with each stat. The idea is you can add up the weight of all the stats on a piece of gear, then compare the total with another piece of gear and use the piece with a higher total stat weight value.

Finding your stat weights

Note that your stat weight's are going to change significantly as time goes on, and depending on what content you are doing. The above values are an approximation. For the most accurate stat weight's go to Wowanalyze there you can plug in a log from Warcraftlogs and it will generate stat weight's for you.
